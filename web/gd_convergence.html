A mode diverges the instant its dot enters the red zone (|f| > 1). The steep mode — largest λ — always gets there first: that is the whole theorem.

Why the slow mode still hurts

Below the cliff, the error shrinks like max|1−αλi|t. The best you can do is α* = 2/(λmin+λmax) (green tick), giving rate (κ−1)/(κ+1) — ill-conditioned bowls are slow even when stable. For linear regression H = ⅟ₙ XᵀX: the data itself sets your budget (PS1 1(e)).
